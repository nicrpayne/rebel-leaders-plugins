# Rebel Leaders Plugins: Full Pipeline Audit

## Purpose of This Document

This document maps the complete journey from the moment a leader answers the first Gravitas question to the moment they read a cartridge on the tablet. Every piece of content, every scoring threshold, and every ranking decision is laid bare so you can audit whether the system faithfully represents your teachings: the four Base Elements, the Enneagram undercurrent, Spiral Dynamics, thinking wavelengths, and the five key areas of health.

---

## Part 1: The Four Base Elements

The entire system orbits four Base Elements. These are the organizational and leadership primitives that form the load-bearing pillars of the Gravitas assessment, the Codex ranking, and the cartridge content.

| Base Element | Guiding Question | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity** | Who are we? | Agency, self-awareness, ownership, authenticity |
| **Relationship** | How do we connect? | Trust, safety, belonging, conflict metabolism |
| **Vision** | Where are we going? | Purpose, direction, resilience, strategic focus |
| **Culture** | How does it feel? | Rituals, energy, norms, the lived environment |

## Part 4: The Side-Chain Handoff (Results → Codex)

When the user clicks "Side-Chain to Codex" on the Results page, the following happens:

1. The bottleneck (lowest Base Element key) and firstMove (archetype name) are encoded into URL params
2. The full scoring result is saved to `localStorage` as `gravityCheckResults`
3. The user navigates to `/codex?signal=received&bottleneck=IDENTITY&firstMove=THE%20OXYGEN%20PROTOCOL`
4. Codex receives the signal, retrieves the full scores from localStorage, builds a `GravitasSignal` object
5. The ranking algorithm scores all 22 cartridges and auto-loads the highest-scoring one into the deck

## Part 5: The Ranking Algorithm (How Cartridges Are Selected)

The ranking algorithm scores each cartridge against the Gravitas signal using six weighted factors:

| Factor | Max Points | How It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Bottleneck Match** | 40 | Does the cartridge's `flywheel_node` include the user's lowest Base Element? This is the primary driver. |
| **Second-Lowest Match** | 3-15 | Does the cartridge also address the second-weakest Base Element? Points scale by how close the second-lowest is to the lowest (within 0.4 = 15 pts, within 0.8 = 8 pts, further = 3 pts). |
| **Leak-Type Overlap** | 20 (cap) | How many of the cartridge's `leak_types` match the semantic map for the user's leak element? Diminishing returns: 8 + 6 + 4 + 2 per match. |
| **Force Overlap** | 12 (cap) | How many of the cartridge's `dominant_forces` match the semantic map for the user's force element? Diminishing returns: 5 + 4 + 3 per match. |
| **Severity-Difficulty Fit** | 10 | Does the cartridge's difficulty match the severity of the problem? Severe scores (< 2.5) prefer difficulty 1-2. Moderate (2.5-3.5) prefer difficulty 2-3. Mild (> 3.5) prefer difficulty 3-5. |
| **Archetype Alignment** | 3 | Light tiebreaker: does the cartridge's difficulty fall within the affinity range for the user's archetype? |

**Maximum possible score: ~100 points.** Cartridges are sorted descending by score, with difficulty as a final tiebreaker (prefer accessible).

## Part 8: Where Your Teachings Need to Show Up

This is the critical audit section. Here is where the Enneagram, Spiral Dynamics, thinking wavelengths, and the five key areas of health should be woven into the system, and an honest assessment of where they currently are and are not.

### 8.1 The Questions

**Current state:** The questions use evocative, metaphorical language ("gravity well," "fridge rights," "shield wall," "antifragile"). The tone is distinctive and not generic corporate. However, the questions do not currently encode or reference:

- **Enneagram types or patterns.** No question targets a specific Enneagram motivation (e.g., the Two's need to be needed, the Eight's need for control, the Five's withdrawal). The questions measure the *environment* and *team dynamics*, not individual type patterns.
- **Spiral Dynamics stages.** No question differentiates between, say, a Blue/Orange team (rules + achievement) and a Green/Yellow team (community + systems). The questions assume a single ideal state rather than meeting the leader where their developmental stage actually is.
- **Thinking wavelengths.** There is no distinction between analytical, emotional, creative, or intuitive processing in the questions. All questions are framed as agree/disagree statements.

**What could change:** The questions could be layered so that certain questions probe Enneagram-adjacent motivations (fear of failure, need for approval, avoidance of conflict, need for control) without naming the Enneagram directly. Spiral Dynamics could inform the *scoring interpretation* rather than the questions themselves, recognizing that a "3.5 in Culture" means something different for a Blue-stage org vs. a Green-stage org. The Base Elements themselves are sound as organizational primitives; the depth needs to come from what the questions probe within each element.

### 8.2 The Scoring and Archetypes

**Current state:** The five archetypes (Dead Orbit through Full Orbit) are severity tiers based on the total score. They describe the gravitational state of the team. The language is strong and original.

**What's missing:** The archetypes don't differentiate *why* a team is at that level. Two teams could both score 2.5 (Friction Belt) for completely different reasons: one because of Enneagram Three leaders performing instead of leading, another because of Enneagram Nine leaders avoiding conflict. The archetype description and first move are the same for both.

**What could change:** The Leak and Force combination could generate more nuanced archetype variants. Instead of 5 archetypes, you could have 5 severity tiers x 4 leak types = 20 distinct readings. The first move could be tailored to the specific leak, not just the severity.

### 8.3 The Cartridge Content

**Current state:** The cartridges are practical, tactical, and well-structured. The "Why It Works" sections reference psychology (Gottman, Edmondson, Sinek) and some field wisdom. The scripts are usable and specific.

**What's missing:**
- **No Enneagram awareness in the scripts or protocols.** A script for "Accountability With Care" doesn't acknowledge that accountability lands differently on a Two (who feels unloved) vs. an Eight (who feels controlled) vs. a Five (who feels invaded).
- **No Spiral Dynamics awareness in the "Use When" or "Avoid" sections.** The advice is stage-agnostic.
- **No thinking wavelength differentiation.** All protocols assume a linear, verbal processing style.
- **The "proof" sections** (research, books, field notes) are present on only the newer cartridges (North Star Sentence, Kill the Ghost Goal, Win Condition, Tradeoff Talk, Permission Slip, Shadow Norms, Energy Leak Check, Safe to Say). The original 16 cartridges have no proof/research section.
- **No reference materials** (videos, writings, links) are populated on any cartridge. The `resources` field exists in the schema but is empty everywhere.

### 8.4 The Five Key Areas of Health (RESOLVED)

**Answer:** The fifth element is **Leadership (Awareness)**. It is not a separate Base Element to score. It is the field that permeates all four orbits. In the orbital model, Leadership appears at every corner of the diagram, not inside any single orbit. It is the meta-layer: the awareness that holds the system together.

The four Base Elements are arranged as concentric orbits, with Identity at the core and Culture as the outermost ring. Leadership (Awareness) is the gravitational field running through all of them.
